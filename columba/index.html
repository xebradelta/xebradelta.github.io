Heritage · why this is a serious idea

Moving money has always been the hard part.

For most of history, the problem was never agreeing on a price — it was getting value across distance without it being robbed, sunk, debased, or delayed past usefulness. Civilisations solved this with trust networks and clever proxies long before they solved it with wires. COLUMBA simply returns to the proven medium.

The bill of exchange

Medieval bankers — the Templars on the pilgrim roads, later the Medici — realised you should never carry the gold. You deposit here, present a sealed note there, and the metal never travels. Settlement became a message. We agree: the message just happens to have wings.

The Reuter pigeon line, 1850

Where the German and Belgian telegraphs didn't yet meet, Paul Reuter flew 45 pigeons between Aachen and Brussels carrying stock prices — two hours a trip, beating the railway by six. A literal gap in the wire, closed by birds. We are, you could say, restoring the original protocol.

Siege of Paris, 1870–71

Surrounded and cut off, Paris shrank its dispatches to microfilm and flew them out by pigeon — thousands of messages on one bird. Proof of the founding COLUMBA insight: a pigeon's value-per-gram, correctly packed, is absurdly high.

Hawala, still running

The hawala system moves value across continents on trust and a phone call — no money physically crosses the border at all. COLUMBA is hawala with a settlement layer you can see flying overhead, which we consider a transparency upgrade.

RFC 1149 / 2549

In 1990 the IETF formally specified IP over Avian Carriers — and in 2001 a Norwegian Linux group actually pinged a host by pigeon. High latency, magnificent bandwidth. The standards body already blessed the medium; we merely added a balance sheet.

Winston v. the ISP, 2009

A South African pigeon named Winston carried a 4 GB stick 100 km in 2h 06m; the country's largest ISP had transmitted 4% of the same data by the time he landed. Sometimes the bird is simply the faster wire. We took the hint.

How the Wire works

Four steps from your hand to theirs.

01

Deposit at a loft

Bring cash to any COLUMBA loft. A bonded loftmaster counts it, photographs the serials, and folds it into a tamper-evident capsule sealed with numbered wax.

02

Banding & briefing

We band a rested, home-loyal bird, log its leg-band to your transfer, and brief it on the destination loft. (Briefing is mostly for our benefit; it already knows the way.)

03

Launch & relay

The bird flies a direct great-circle route. Over long corridors it hands off at relay lofts — fresh wings, same capsule, unbroken seal — exactly as the medieval relays did.

04

Arrival & settlement

At the destination loft the wax is checked against the number, the capsule is opened before two witnesses, and your recipient is paid in local cash. Seal intact = settled.

Errors, delays & incidents · the honest part

Things that go wrong, and what we do about them.

A serious settlement network plans for failure. Here is our incident taxonomy — the codes that may appear on your Bird-Trace, what they mean, and the remedy. Most are recoverable. None are your fault.

ERR HAWK-409

Interception event

A bird of prey engaged the courier mid-route ("the wire was tapped"). Capsule recovery dispatched; payment re-issued from the predation reserve. Premium tiers fly hawk-screened corridors.

ERR FOG-503

Network congestion (weather)

Low visibility grounds the flock — pigeons navigate by landmark and sun. Held at the last loft until skies clear. Settlement clock pauses; no funds at risk.

ERR WIND-429

Headwind throttling

Sustained headwinds cut ground speed and force an extra rest stop. ETA extends; the bird is fine and possibly building character.

ERR MOLT-307

Seasonal redirect

Bird entered molt and cannot fly distance. Capsule transferred to a fresh courier at the loft. Common late summer; build it into your timeline.

ERR PAYLOAD-413

Capsule too heavy

Requested sum exceeds single-bird payload. Auto-split into a multi-bird flight (or escalated to St. Bernard / camel). All sub-capsules must land to settle.

ERR CRUMB-302

Distraction / off-route

Courier diverted to a food source ("a better offer"). Usually self-correcting within the hour. Repeat offenders are reassigned to the snail tier.

ERR LOFT-404

Destination loft not found

Recipient loft unregistered or relocated. Bird returns home with capsule and seal intact — funds bounce back to sender, never lost in flight.

ERR SEAL-401

Wax tamper detected

Capsule arrived with a broken or mismatched seal. Settlement frozen, transfer quarantined, investigation opened. No payout until the seal number reconciles.

ERR COO-204

Settled — no action needed

The happy path. Seal verified, witnesses signed, recipient paid. Your bird is home, fed, and resting for the next wire.

Trust, security & compliance
Every gram is sealed, numbered, photographed, and flown by a bird with a documented record of coming home. We call our onboarding Know Your Coop — and yes, the seal number is the whole point.

Know Your Coop (KYC)

Senders and recipient lofts are registered and bonded. We verify the loft, not just the bird — a bird can't abscond if it has nowhere unfamiliar to land.

Tamper-evident settlement

Numbered wax seal + serial photography + two-witness opening. A broken seal freezes the transfer automatically. Funds bounce home before they're ever lost.

Predation reserve

A pooled fund makes whole any capsule lost to hawks, weather, or off-route events on covered tiers. The bird's bad day is not your loss.

Questions, reasonably asked

The things everyone wonders.

Is this real? Can I actually send money?
No. COLUMBA is a satirical concept piece — an affectionate thought experiment about how value moved before wires, dressed up as a fintech. It does not accept deposits, dispatch birds, or move one real cent. Please do not strap cash to a pigeon and expect this site to honour it.
How much can one pigeon carry?
A homing pigeon comfortably carries roughly 10% of its body weight — about 50 g — over distance. In large notes that's ~50 bills; in gold leaf, considerably more value; in coin, almost nothing. The calculator auto-splits anything heavier across multiple birds or escalates to a pack-animal tier.
What happens if a hawk gets my bird?
On covered tiers, the predation reserve re-issues your payment and we dispatch capsule recovery. Premium tiers fly hawk-screened corridors to reduce the risk. It's the avian equivalent of someone tapping the wire — planned for, insured, recoverable.
Why is the falcon faster but more expensive?
A peregrine in a stoop is the fastest animal alive (300+ km/h), so it settles urgent transfers same-day. It's pricier because of hawk-screened routing, no rest stops, and the operational discipline of never, under any circumstance, routing it near our pigeon flock.
Is the Tortoise Vault a joke?
It's the most serious tier, actually. Slow, armoured, and nearly impossible to rob in a hurry — cold storage that happens to be ambling toward its destination. If you don't need the money this decade, it's the cheapest and safest option we have.
Has money-by-bird ever genuinely worked?
Information-by-bird absolutely has — Reuter's 1850 stock-price pigeons, the microfilm post out of besieged Paris, the IETF's IP-over-Avian-Carriers standard, and Winston the pigeon out-running an ISP in 2009. Value is just information with a seal on it. That's the premise we're playing with.

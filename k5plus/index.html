Baofeng K5 Plus
manual programming guide

Everything you need to program the K5 Plus by hand, from the keypad — no computer, no cable, no CHIRP. Built as an installable offline app: add it to your phone’s home screen and the whole guide works with no signal.

Works offline once loaded

Licensing & legal Transmitting on amateur, GMRS, or business frequencies generally requires a licence in your country (in the US: an FCC amateur or GMRS licence). The K5 Plus can tune and transmit outside bands you may legally use. You are responsible for transmitting only on frequencies and at power levels you are authorised for. When in doubt, use the lowest power needed and consult your local regulator or radio club. This guide is educational and unofficial.

Overview

What the K5 Plus is

The Baofeng K5 Plus (sold as part of the 5RM / 5RH “PLUS” series, and closely related to the UV-5RM Plus) is a dual-band/dual-watch handheld transceiver with a colour LCD, USB-C charging, NOAA weather reception, and a wideband scanning receiver. It stores 999 memory channels, each holding receive/transmit frequencies, power level, bandwidth, tone (CTCSS/DCS) and signalling settings, and a name. [1][3]

Specifications from the official 5RM/5RH (UV-5RM Plus / K5 Plus) user manual, Appendix “Technical Specifications.” [1]

M vs L versions The “M” version supports AM 108–136 MHz (air band) scanning and a voice-scramble function; the “L” version does not, and has no scramble item in its menu. [1]

Radio tour

Buttons, controls & the display

Get comfortable with these names — every guide below refers to them.

① Antenna (SMA, removable) ② SK1 — FM broadcast / alarm ③ PTT — push-to-talk ④ SK2 — flashlight / monitor ⑤ Colour LCD ⑥ Status LED (red=TX, green=RX) ⑦ Numeric keypad ⑧ MENU (confirm) / EXIT (back) ⑨ ▲ / ▼ navigation keys

Original illustration recreated from the control layout described in the official manual. [1]

What each key does

Power / Volume knob

Rotate clockwise from a “click” to turn on; keep turning for volume. A double-beep confirms power-on.

PTT

Press and hold to transmit on the active (selected) display.

MENU key

Opens the menu, selects an item, and confirms a setting. (Shown as MENU throughout.)

EXIT key

Backs out of the menu without saving the current change / returns to standby.

▲ / ▼ keys

Step frequency or channel up/down, change a menu value, or change scan direction. Hold ~2 s to move rapidly.

VFO/MR key

Switches between Frequency (VFO) mode and Channel (MR) mode.

A/B key

Selects the upper (A) or lower (B) display as the active one. Save channels from the A display.

SK1 (top side key)

Short press toggles broadcast FM radio; also used for the alarm function.

SK2 (lower side key)

Flashlight; hold to enter Monitor (open squelch to hear weak signals).

✱ (Star) key

Short press = reverse (swap TX/RX). Hold ~2 s = lock / unlock the keypad.

# (Pound) key

Activates DTMF dial; hold to start scan; auto-searches stations in FM mode.

0 / SQL key

Hold the 0 key to turn on NOAA weather reception.

Key functions per the manual’s “Radio Overview” and “Main keypad controls.” [1]

The basics

VFO vs. Channel, and how the menu works

Almost all manual programming happens in Frequency (VFO) mode; you then save the result into a numbered memory channel for everyday use in Channel (MR) mode.

Frequency mode — VFO

For tuning any frequency directly: testing, field setup, and building a channel before you store it.

Channel mode — MR

For selecting the pre-programmed channels you saved. Day-to-day operating mode.

Switch between them with the VFO/MR key.

Working the menu (and the shortcut trick)

Every menu item has a number (0–46). You can jump straight to one instead of scrolling:

MENU→ 29type the item number→ MENUto edit→ ▲▼change value→ MENUsave→ EXIT
The pattern to memorise Open menu → key the item number → MENU to edit → ▲/▼ to choose → MENU to save → EXIT to leave. The ten most common items sit at the top of the list. [1]

Guide 01

Enter a frequency directly (VFO)

Example: tune the 2 m calling frequency 146.520 MHz on display A.

  1. Press VFO/MR until the VFO icon shows — you’re in Frequency mode.
  2. Press A/B if needed so the A (upper) display is active.
    ___._____A ◀ VFO
  3. Key the digits on the numeric keypad — no decimal point needed:
    146520
    146.52000A ◀ VFO
  4. That’s it — the radio is tuned. Use ▲/▼ to nudge up/down by your current step (Menu 1).
Tip If digits land in the wrong place, your step setting (Menu 1) determines how many decimals are accepted. For most ham use set step to 5.0 k or 12.5 k. To enter 446.00625 you’d need a 6.25 k step. [1]

Guide 02

Store a simplex channel (with a tone)

Worked example straight from the manual: save 432.6625 MHz with a transmit CTCSS tone of 123.0 Hz into channel 10. [1]

  1. Press VFO/MR to enter VFO mode; make sure the A display is active (A/B).
  2. Clear any old data in channel 10 first — open Menu 31 (DELCH) and delete it:
    MENU31→MENU→10→MENU
  3. Set the transmit tone — Menu 12 (T-CTCS) → choose 123.0:
    MENU12→MENU→▲▼to 123.0→MENU
  4. Back in VFO, key the frequency:
    4326625
    432.66250A ◀ VFO CT
  5. Store it into channel 10 — Menu 30 (MEMCH):
    MENU30→MENU→10→MENUsaved
  6. Press VFO/MR to return to MR mode — channel 10 now appears.
    432.66250CH 010
    MRCT 123.0
About names Channel names can only be entered with PC software (CHIRP or the Baofeng programmer) — the keypad stores the frequency and settings, not text. Set Menu 24/25 (MDF-A/B) to FREQ or CH to choose what the display shows. [1]

Guide 03

Store a repeater channel (split TX/RX)

This is the “store the RX and TX legs into the same channel” method from the manual. Example: RX 432.550, TX 437.550 (a +5 MHz split) with TX CTCSS 123.0 into channel 10. [1]

  1. Enter VFO mode (VFO/MR); make the A display active.
  2. Delete old data in channel 10 — Menu 31:
    MENU31→MENU→10→MENU
  3. Set the TX tone — Menu 12 (T-CTCS) → 123.0 → save.
  4. Key the receive frequency (the repeater’s output / what you listen to):
    4325500
  5. Save it as the RX leg of channel 10 — Menu 30 (MEMCH) → 10 → MENU.
    “RX has been added.”
  6. Now key the transmit frequency (the repeater’s input):
    4375500
  7. Save it to the same channel 10 again — Menu 30 → 10 → MENU.
    “TX has been added.” The radio now knows: receive 432.550, transmit 437.550.
  8. Press VFO/MR to go back to MR — channel 10 is your repeater.
    432.55000CH 010
    MR+ splitCT 123.0
Why two saves? Storing the RX frequency, then the TX frequency, into the same channel number teaches the channel its split directly — handy for odd splits that aren’t a standard ± offset. For standard offsets, the VFO method below is faster.

Guide 04

Program a repeater the quick way (offset + shift)

If your repeater uses a standard offset (e.g. +5 MHz on 70 cm, −0.6 MHz on 2 m), set the shift direction and offset amount, then save. [1]

  1. Enter VFO mode and key the repeater’s output (receive) frequency on the keypad.
  2. Set the offset amount — Menu 29 (OFFSET):
    MENU29→MENU→key the offset, e.g.05.000→MENU
    Offset range 00.000–69.990 MHz in 10 kHz steps.
  3. Set the shift direction — Menu 28 (SFT-D) → + (TX higher) or − (TX lower):
    MENU28→MENU→▲▼+ / − / OFF→MENU
  4. Set the TX tone the repeater needs — Menu 12 (T-CTCS) → your tone → save.
  5. Press EXIT. Test by keying up: status LED goes red on TX. The display shows + or − for the offset.
  6. Happy with it? Save the whole thing into a channel — Menu 30 (MEMCH) → channel number → MENU.
Listen on the input Press the ✱ (reverse) key to temporarily swap TX/RX and hear the repeater’s input frequency — an R appears in the display. Press again to undo. [1]
Tone-burst repeaters Some repeaters (common in Europe) need a 1750 Hz tone burst. Set Menu 37 (TONE) to 1750, then press PTT + SK2 to send it. [1]

Guide 05

CTCSS & DCS tones (sub-audible squelch)

Tones let a repeater (or another radio) decide whether to act on your signal, and let your radio stay quiet until it hears a matching tone.

Menu 12 · T-CTCS

Transmit CTCSS — the analogue tone you send to open a repeater. The one you usually need.

Menu 13 · T-DCS

Transmit DCS — the digital coded version. Use only if your repeater specifies DCS.

Menu 10 · R-CTCS

Receive CTCSS — your radio only un-mutes when it hears this tone. Leave OFF unless you know you need it.

Menu 11 · R-DCS

Receive DCS — digital receive squalch. Leave OFF unless required.

Set a transmit tone

MENU12→MENU→▲▼pick tone (e.g. 88.5)→MENU→EXIT
Common mistake Setting a receive tone (Menu 10/11) when you only meant to set a transmit tone (Menu 12/13) is the usual reason a beginner “can’t hear anything.” If your radio went silent, check Menu 10 and 11 are OFF. [1]
Scan for an unknown tone In VFO mode you can scan to find a tone a station is using: Menu 14 (Scan CTCSS) or Menu 15 (Scan DCS), then press MENU. This only works in VFO mode, not channel mode. [1]

Guide 06

Power, bandwidth, step & squelch

The four settings you’ll touch most often. Same pattern every time: MENU → number → MENU → ▲▼ → MENU.

Transmit power — Menu 2 (TXP)HIGH or LOW. Use the lowest power that does the job.
Bandwidth — Menu 5 (W/N)WIDE = 25 kHz, NARR = 12.5 kHz. Match the channel’s plan; most modern channels are narrow.
Step — Menu 1 (STEP)2.5 / 5.0 / 6.25 / 10 / 12.5 / 20 / 25 / 50 kHz. Controls keypad entry resolution and ▲▼ jumps.
Squelch — Menu 0 (SQL)0–9. 0 = fully open (hear everything incl. noise); higher = quieter, but may miss weak signals. 3–5 is typical.
Fast power toggle There’s a quick high/low power shortcut on the keypad as well — check Menu 2 to confirm what’s active. For an instant “hear the weak stuff” override, hold SK2 for Monitor (temporarily opens squelch). [1]

Guide 07

Scanning

Scan frequencies (VFO mode)

  1. In VFO mode, hold the # key (or hold a navigation key) for ~2 seconds — scanning starts by your current step.
  2. Change direction with ▲/▼.
  3. Press any key (or EXIT) to stop.

Scan channels (MR mode)

  1. In MR mode, hold # for ~2 seconds to scan your stored channels.
  2. Use ▲/▼ for direction; press a key to stop.
How scan resumes — Menu 21 (SC-REV)
TO (time): resumes after a fixed pause. CO (carrier): resumes when the signal drops. SE (search): stops and stays. [1]

Guide 08

Dual watch (monitor A and B)

Dual watch lets the radio listen to both the A and B displays and jump to whichever becomes active.

  1. Open the menu and go to Menu 7 (TDR):
    MENU7→MENU
  2. Use ▲/▼ to choose ON (or OFF).
  3. Press MENU to confirm, then EXIT.
Note When two channels show, press A/B to choose which one is the active TX/RX display. Save channels only from the A display. [1]

Guide 09

Copy a frequency over the air

The K5 Plus can grab a nearby radio’s frequency and tone automatically — handy when someone reads you in but you don’t know their settings. [2]

  1. Long-press the BAND button on the K5 Plus. The display shows “SEARCH.”
  2. Have the other radio transmit the frequency you want to copy.
  3. The K5 Plus searches and displays the detected frequency and any privacy (CTCSS/DCS) code.
  4. Press MENU to save the captured frequency.
Heads-up This “one-click copy” behaviour is documented by the retailer / user community rather than the core manual; exact button labels can vary slightly by firmware. If BAND isn’t labelled, try the side keys. [2]

Guide 10

NOAA weather reception

  1. From standby, press and hold the 0 (SQL) key to turn on NOAA weather reception.
  2. The radio tunes the weather band; it will receive the seven NOAA channels (162.400–162.550 MHz).
WX-01162.550 MHz
WX-02162.400 MHz
WX-03162.475 MHz
WX-04162.425 MHz
WX-05162.450 MHz
WX-06162.500 MHz
WX-07162.525 MHz

Weather channels are receive-only. Channel mapping per the manual’s weather table. [1]

Guide 11

Delete a memory channel

  1. Open Menu 31 (DELCH):
    MENU31→MENU
  2. Key the channel number to erase (001–999), e.g. 010.
  3. Press MENU to confirm the deletion, then EXIT.
Good habit Always DELCH a channel before re-programming it, so leftover tone/offset settings don’t carry over. [1]

Guide 12

Factory reset

When the radio is misbehaving and you want a clean slate, use Menu 42 (RESET).

  1. Open Menu 42:
    MENU42→MENU
  2. Choose the scope with ▲/▼:
    VFOsettings only
    ALLeverything + channels
  3. Press MENU to confirm. The radio shows a “Sure to reset” style prompt, then powers off and reboots. [1]
Warning ALL erases your stored memory channels too. Back up with PC software first if your channels matter. VFO resets only the working settings and leaves channels intact.

FAQ

My radio went silent — I can’t hear anything. What happened?

Almost always a receive tone left switched on. Check Menu 10 (R-CTCS) and Menu 11 (R-DCS) and set both to OFF. A receive tone mutes the speaker until it hears that exact tone, so an ordinary signal seems to vanish. Also check squelch isn’t set too high (Menu 0) and that the volume knob is up. Hold SK2 for Monitor to confirm the radio is actually hearing RF. [1]

Can I name my channels from the keypad?

No — alphanumeric channel names can only be entered with PC software (CHIRP or Baofeng’s programmer over the USB-C/programming cable). From the keypad you store the frequency, tone, power, bandwidth and offset. Set Menu 24/25 (MDF-A/B) to FREQ or CH so the display shows something useful. [1]

I keyed a frequency but the last digits won’t go in.

Your step (Menu 1) sets the entry resolution. To enter something like 446.00625 you need a 6.25 kHz step; for 145.7625 you need 12.5 kHz. Set the matching step first, then re-enter the frequency. [1]

I can hear the repeater but can’t key it up.

Three things to check: (1) the offset direction (Menu 28) and amount (Menu 29) match the repeater; (2) the transmit tone (Menu 12 CTCSS or 13 DCS) is exactly what the repeater requires; (3) you’re actually transmitting on the input — press ✱ reverse to confirm the input frequency is right. Some repeaters need a 1750 Hz tone burst (Menu 37 + PTT + SK2). [1]

What’s the difference between VFO and MR (Channel) mode?

VFO (Frequency mode) is for tuning any frequency directly and building a channel. MR (Channel mode) is for selecting channels you’ve already saved. Toggle with VFO/MR. You program in VFO, then save with Menu 30 and operate in MR. [1]

How do I reset everything to factory defaults?

Menu 42 (RESET). VFO resets working settings only; ALL resets everything and erases your stored channels. Back up first if your channels matter. [1]

The keypad locked itself / a key won’t respond.

Auto keypad lock (Menu 27) may be on. Hold the ✱ key for about 2 seconds to lock/unlock. Set Menu 27 to OFF if you don’t want auto-lock. [1]

Is it legal to transmit with this radio?

Depends entirely on the frequency, your location, and your licence. The K5 Plus can transmit on frequencies you may not be authorised to use. In the US, amateur 2 m/70 cm needs an FCC amateur licence; GMRS needs a GMRS licence; FRS/MURS have their own rules and this radio generally isn’t certified for them. Transmit only where you’re licensed and authorised. [1]

Does this app need internet? Where’s my data going?

No. Once the page has loaded once, it’s cached on your device by a service worker and runs fully offline — install it to your home screen and open it in airplane mode. It makes no network calls and collects nothing; everything is static HTML.

How do I install this as an app?

Android/Chrome: tap the ⤓ Install button in the top bar, or the browser’s “Add to Home screen.” iPhone/Safari: Share → “Add to Home Screen.” Desktop Chrome/Edge: the install icon in the address bar, or the ⤓ Install button. After that it opens like a normal app and works offline.

What about CHIRP / computer programming?

This guide focuses on manual keypad programming. For bulk channel loading, names, and backups, the open-source CHIRP software with a USB-C programming cable is the usual route — and the manual recommends reading the radio’s factory data first, then editing and writing it back. [1]

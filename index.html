What this is

Tools that come from somewhere real

Xebra Delta is a collection of things built in response to actual moments. A gap noticed. A frustration felt. A need that existed and hadn't been addressed yet — or just an idea that seemed interesting enough to act on.

There's no product roadmap. There's no unified vision of what these tools are supposed to be collectively. Each one comes from wherever it comes from.

What gets made

Mostly practical. Sometimes random.

A lot of what ends up here lives somewhere in the orbit of public safety, emergency management, and 911 — fields that tend to be underserved by the tools actually available to the people working in them.

But sometimes it's something else entirely. That's fine too.

Guiding idea
The tools here are a response — to how things feel in the moment, to something that's needed, or sometimes just to the simple fact that building it seemed worth doing.
01

Reactive, not planned

Nothing here was conceived as part of a suite. Something prompts it — a gap, a frustration, a curiosity — and it gets built. That's the whole process.

02

No house style

There's no shared aesthetic or approach applied across everything. Each tool looks and works the way it needs to for what it is. Consistency isn't the goal.

03

Made to be useful

If something is shared here, it's because it might actually help someone. Not to demonstrate a technique or check a box. Because it works and someone might need it.

The short version

Sometimes the best reason to make something is that you needed it and it didn't exist. That's usually enough.

These tools are released openly. Take them, use them, adapt them. No ceremony required.

Approach

Built because, not in order to

The distinction matters. Tools built because something was needed tend to be more honest than tools built to fulfil a strategy. They're also usually simpler — they solve the specific thing they were made to solve and don't try to be more than that.

Scope

No unified scope. By design.

Emergency management, 911, public safety — these show up a lot because that's the work that generates the needs. But the scope is really just: whatever came up. What ends up here reflects what was worth making at the time.
